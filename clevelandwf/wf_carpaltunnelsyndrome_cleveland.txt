CARPAL TUNNEL SYNDROME TREATMENT WORKFLOW (from Cleveland Clinic-based plan)

1. Initial conservative management
   - Wear a neutral-position wrist splint, especially at night.
   - Modify daily activities and ergonomics to reduce repetitive wrist flexion/extension.
   - Use OTC pain relievers (acetaminophen or NSAIDs) short-term as advised.
   - Begin physical/occupational therapy for stretching, strengthening and posture adjustment.

2. Escalation of nonsurgical care
   - If symptoms persist, consider corticosteroid injection into the carpal tunnel.
   - Reassess contributing conditions (diabetes, thyroid disease, inflammatory arthritis).

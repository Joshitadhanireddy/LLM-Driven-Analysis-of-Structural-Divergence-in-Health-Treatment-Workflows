MARFAN SYNDROME MANAGEMENT WORKFLOW (from Mayo Clinic-based plan)

1. Overall strategy
   - No cure; aim to prevent complications and monitor progression.
   - Regular follow-up for heart, eyes, and skeleton.

2. Cardiovascular management
   - Blood pressure–lowering medicines to:
     * Prevent aortic enlargement.
     * Reduce risk of dissection/rupture.
   - Lifelong monitoring of aortic size.

3. Ophthalmologic management
   - Correct dislocated lens–related vision problems with glasses or contacts.
   - Consider surgery for:
     * Retinal tears or detachment.
     * Cataracts (lens replacement).

4. Surgical and orthopedic procedures
   - Aortic repair
     * Replace enlarged aortic segment (≈≥50 mm) with synthetic graft.
     * Aortic valve replacement may be needed.
   - Scoliosis treatment
     * Spine specialist evaluation.
     * Bracing and/or surgery in significant curves.
   - Breastbone (sternal) deformities
     * Surgery to correct sunken or protruding chest (often considered cosmetic).

5. Long-term outlook
   - With regular monitoring and modern therapy, many live near-normal lifespans.

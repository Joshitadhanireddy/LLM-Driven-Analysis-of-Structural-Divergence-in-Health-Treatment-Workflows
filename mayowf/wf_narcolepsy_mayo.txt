NARCOLEPSY MANAGEMENT WORKFLOW (from Mayo Clinic-based plan)

1. Overall approach
   - No cure; manage symptoms with medicines + lifestyle changes.
   - Screen for drug interactions and sedating OTC meds (e.g., cold/allergy).

2. Wake-promoting / stimulant medicines
   - First-line newer stimulants:
     * Modafinil, armodafinil.
     * Solriamfetol, pitolisant (also helpful for cataplexy).
   - Traditional stimulants (second-line):
     * Methylphenidate, amphetamines.
     * Monitor for dependence, nervousness, tachycardia.

3. Cataplexy and REM-related symptom control
   - SSRIs/SNRIs:
     * Venlafaxine, fluoxetine, duloxetine, sertraline.
   - Tricyclic antidepressants:
     * Protriptyline, imipramine, clomipramine.
   - Sodium oxybate / oxybate salts:
     * Xyrem, Lumryz, Xywav.
     * Improve nocturnal sleep and cataplexy; may reduce daytime sleepiness.
     * Use cautiously due to risk of respiratory depression, especially with other sedatives or alcohol.

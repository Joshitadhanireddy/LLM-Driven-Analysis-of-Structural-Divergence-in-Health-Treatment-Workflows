ATOPIC DERMATITIS (ECZEMA) MANAGEMENT WORKFLOW (from Mayo Clinic-based plan)

1. First-line: Skin care & moisturization
   - Daily moisturizers; avoid irritants & temperature extremes.
   - Apply meds first → then moisturizer.

2. Medications
   2.1 Topicals
       - Corticosteroid creams/ointments (various strengths).
       - Calcineurin inhibitors (tacrolimus, pimecrolimus) — avoid strong sunlight.
   2.2 Infection control
       - Oral antibiotics if lesions infected.
   2.3 Systemic anti‑inflammatory meds (severe cases)
       - Cyclosporine, methotrexate, prednisone, mycophenolate, azathioprine.
   2.4 Biologic options
       - Dupilumab (age >6), tralokinumab (adults).

3. Therapies
   - Wet wrap therapy (hospital or supervised home use).
   - Light therapy (NB-UVB, UVA), used when topicals fail.
   - Counseling for psychosocial impact.
   - Relaxation / behavior modification / biofeedback for habitual scratching.

4. Infant eczema
   - Avoid irritants; short warm baths; moisturize while damp.
   - If no improvement → prescription topicals or oral antihistamines (night use).

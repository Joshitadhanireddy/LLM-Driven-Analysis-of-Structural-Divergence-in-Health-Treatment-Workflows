BELL’S PALSY MANAGEMENT WORKFLOW (from Mayo Clinic-based plan)

1. Core principles
   - Most recover fully.
   - Protect the eye on affected side.
   - Use medicines and physical therapy to improve recovery speed.

2. Eye care
   - Artificial tears during daytime.
   - Eye ointment at night.
   - Protective glasses/goggles by day; eye patch at night.

3. Medications
   - Corticosteroids (prednisone) — most effective when started early.
   - Antivirals (valacyclovir or acyclovir) may be added to steroids in severe cases.
   - Antivirals alone are not effective.

4. Physical therapy
   - Facial massage and muscle exercises.
   - Prevent muscle atrophy and contracture.

5. Surgery
   - Decompression surgery NOT recommended.
   - Rarely: plastic surgery for long-term facial asymmetry (eyelid lift, implants, nerve grafts).

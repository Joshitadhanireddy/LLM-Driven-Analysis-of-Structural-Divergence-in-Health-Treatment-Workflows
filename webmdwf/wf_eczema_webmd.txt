ATOPIC DERMATITIS (ECZEMA) MANAGEMENT WORKFLOW (from WebMD-based plan)

1. Trigger avoidance
   - Identify triggers: stress, sweat, chemicals, dust, pollen, foods (kids).
   - Protect skin in cold/dry weather; avoid harsh soaps; rinse laundry thoroughly.  fileciteturn10file3

2. Home skin care
   - Mild soap; daily moisturization after bathing.
   - Short warm (not hot) showers.
   - Humidifier in dry environments.
   - Bleach baths weekly for severe eczema (kills skin bacteria).  fileciteturn10file3

3. Medications
   - Hydrocortisone for mild eczema; stronger steroids for severe cases.
   - Antihistamines for itching.
   - Immune‑modulating meds (tacrolimus, pimecrolimus, crisaborole).
   - Systemic immunosuppressants for severe flares (azathioprine, cyclosporine, methotrexate).
   - Injectables: dupilumab, tralokinumab, lebrikizumab, nemolizumab; oral JAK inhibitors (rinvoq, abrocitinib).  fileciteturn10file3

4. Phototherapy
   - UV therapy for adult severe eczema; discuss risks (aging, skin cancer).  fileciteturn10file3

5. Infant eczema care
   - Avoid fragranced products.
   - Trim nails, use gloves.
   - Bath–moisturize–apply medications routine.
   - Wet wrap therapy for severe cases.  fileciteturn10file3
